Threats of Cyberpsychosis

What is Cyberpsychosis?

Definition: Cyberpsychosis is a debilitating condition that arises from excessive cybernetic augmentation, causing individuals to lose their grip on reality.

Symptoms: Those affected may experience severe psychological disturbances, hallucinations, and unpredictable, often violent behavior.

Causes: The primary cause is the overuse of cybernetic implants and enhancements, which disrupt the delicate balance between the human mind and artificial components.

The Threat of Cyberpsychosis

Impact on Individuals: Victims of cyberpsychosis can transform from everyday citizens into dangerous entities, posing a threat to themselves and those around them.

Public Safety: The unpredictable nature of cyberpsychosis incidents creates a significant risk for public safety, leading to chaos and fear within the community.

Technological Dependence: As society becomes more reliant on cybernetic enhancements, the prevalence of cyberpsychosis threatens to undermine the very foundation of technological progress.

Societal Consequences: The widespread fear of cyberpsychosis can lead to social unrest, stigmatization of augmented individuals, and a potential rollback of technological advancements.

Ded_l0ck's Mission

Amidst this crisis, Pramesh Chitrakar, aka 'ded_l0ck,' steps up to uncover the truth behind the rising cases of cyberpsychosis and to combat the forces exploiting this vulnerability.
